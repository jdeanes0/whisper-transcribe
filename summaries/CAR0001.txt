**ASSESSMENT:**  
Based on the patient's presentation of sharp chest pain exacerbated by lying down and deep breathing, along with associated symptoms of lightheadedness, trouble breathing, and tachycardia, a preliminary assessment could include a concern for potential cardiac issues or pulmonary involvement.

**PLAN:**  
1. Given the severity and nature of the chest pain, consider immediate cardiac monitoring and further evaluation for possible myocardial infarction.
2. Conduct an electrocardiogram (ECG) to assess cardiac function and rule out acute coronary syndrome.
3. Perform a chest X-ray to evaluate for any pulmonary abnormalities.
4. Discuss smoking cessation strategies given the patient's significant smoking history and current symptoms.
5. Provide counseling on moderation of alcohol consumption and the impact on cardiovascular health.
6. Re-evaluate the patient's diet to ensure a more balanced and healthier eating pattern.
7. Follow up with the patient after initial tests and investigations to determine the appropriate course of action.
